F. A. Q.


Q. Who can donate blood?

A. Any healthy person, 18 to 60 years old with a weight of at least 45 kg. Can donate blood. Please do it today!


Q. How long will it take to donate blood?

A. The actual blood donation takes about 4-6 minutes. The whole donation process from the time you can walk in until the time you leave usually takes 30 minutes, including a medical checkup and refreshments!


Q. Is it safe to donate blood?

A. Yes it is!

Sterile, disposable needles are used for each donor. thus there is no risk of any infection including AIDS Some people believe that they will feel weak after donating blood, but this is unlikely to happen. Just eat a small snack and drink plenty of water before and after the donation.


Q. where can I go to donate blood?

A. you can donate blood at a donation camp near your home, workplace or school, or you can go to a recognized blood bank in your area. Or call the local Indian red cross blood bank for more information.


Q. how many times can I donate blood in a year?

A. you can donate blood every three months. It takes only 48 hours for your body fluids and 21 days for cells to be completely replenished. After three months your body has had more than enough time to re-coup from your earlier donation!


Q. who needs the blood that I donate?

A. Blood is needed regularly for patients with diseases such as Thalassemia,cancer and hemophilia, and also for the treatment of injuries after an accident, major surgeries, anemia, child birth etc.


Q. Why is blood so important?

A. blood is the essence of life. It consists of red cells that transport oxygen, white cells that fight infections, platelets to help in blood clotting, and plasma that carries nutrients to the body.


Q. you wouldn’t want my blood. I am of a common group?

A. we constantly need donors of all blood groups.


Q. no body has ever asked me to donate blood?

A. Consider yourself invited.


Q. I am too old!

A. if you are between 17 and 60 years & in good health you can donate!


Q. but I am underweight!

A. if your weight is more than 45 kg and you are in good health you can donate blood.


Q. oh! But I am anaemic!

A. we test for anaemia before every donation.


Q. how much blood do we have?

A. for men 76 ml per kg of body weight and for women 66 ml per kg of body weight. 50 ml per kg of body weight is needed in circulatory system. Rest is surplus.


Q. how much quantity of blood can one donate?

A. up to 8 ml per kg of body weight that is just a fraction of one’s surplus blood.


Q. what are the constituents of blood?

A. 45% cells, 55% plasma out of which 92% is water.


Q. how long can blood be preserved in blood banks?

A. 35 days at present.


Q. what is the responsibility of a voluntary donor?

A. To provide correct information about one’s health status before blood donation.


Q. what tests do blood bank perform after blood collection in blood banks?

A. blood group, malaria, jaundice, syphilis, and AIDS tests. Normally diseases are not found in the blood of true voluntary blood donors. That is why blood from voluntary blood donors is safe.


Q. what is the annual demand and supply of blood in the country?

A. the annual demand was 80 lac units in the year 2007, whereas 60 lac units were collected from voluntary blood donors. The gap between demand and supply can be bridged by regular voluntary, non-remunerated blood donors.
