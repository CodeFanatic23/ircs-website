INDIAN RED CROSS SOCIETY IN BLOOD TRANSFUSION SERVICES




Indian Red Cross Society has been running the largest Voluntary Blood Banks in India since 1962 and at present contributing 10% of the total blood collection through its 110 blood bank centers across the country. Blood and Blood products are issued free of cost to the Thalassemic and Haemophilic patients and to Government hospitals.


The IRCS has 35 State/Union Territory branches with their more than 700 districts/sub districts branches & is running 110 blood banks across 14 states (as per data- March 2007). During the year 2005-2006, the IRCS Blood Banks of the country conducted 3999 camps and collected 5,12,973 units of Blood out of which 3,45229 units (68%) are voluntary and remaining 1,677,44 units (32%) are replacement donors. The Indian Red Cross Society is planning to establish a network of all Red Cross Blood Banks and to ensure effective coordination, efficient functioning and usefulness in times of disasters.


The Society is fully equipped to collect blood within the premises of the Blood Bank and also has mobile teams, which go out regularly to hold Blood Donation Camps. The mobile team covers different parts of Delhi, Gurgaon, Ghaziabad, Faridabad, Ambala, Bahadurgarh and even goes up to Pilani. Blood Donation Camps are organized in Schools, Colleges and other places with the help of religious, social, political, commercial, and military / Para military organizations. Motivational talks are also arranged to explain to the public, the harmlessness of Blood Donation and the imbalance / gap in demand and supply of blood in the country. The Red Cross also maintains Registers of voluntary and replacement donors. Thus it has a life saving network of many rare blood group donors, who can provide blood to those in need in emergencies.


After collection, the blood is tested for HIV 1& 2, HBs Ag, VDRL & HCV. To ensure effective utilization of blood, besides supplying whole blood, Red Cross also provides its users with the facility of component separation like Fresh frozen plasma, Platelet rich plasma, Platelet Concentrate, Packed Cells and Cryoprecipitate.


Out of a total blood collection of approx. 30,000 units per year, approx 50- 60% goes to the Thalassaemic patients and approx 30-40% to Govt. hospitals free of service charges. The rest approx.20% is provided to the Private hospitals / Nursing Homes at nominal charges of Rs. 500 per unit as per the instructions of the Ministry of Health and Family Welfare.


In 2006, IRCS (NHQ) Blood Bank collected 20,000 blood units from Voluntary Donors (64.3%) from 254 Blood Donation Camps and collected 11080 units of Blood from Replacement Donors (35.7%).


In 2007, IRCS (NHQ) Blood Bank collected 20,584 blood units from Voluntary Donors (80.41%) from 238 Blood Donation Camps and collected 5012 units of Blood from Replacement Donors (19.58%).
