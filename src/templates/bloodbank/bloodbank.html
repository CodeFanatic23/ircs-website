Red Cross was initiated as a movement in 1863 to provide succour to the war affected soldiers, though it is now identified as a humanitarian organization named as Indian Red Cross Society.

It participates actively in the community welfare programmes in the pre- disaster phase and performs initiatives like relief work and voluntary blood donation during the disasters.

It has been running the largest Voluntary Blood Banks in India since 1962 and at present contributing 10% of the total blood collection through its 108 blood bank centers across the country. Blood and Blood products are issued free of cost to the Thalassemic and Haemophilic patients and to Government hospitals.
